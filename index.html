Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Meet the Spider-People from across the Multiverse

Miles Morales

“Sometimes I feel like I’m the only one standing up for what’s right.”

A bright, creative teenager discovering his own identity as he learns to become Spider-Man. Miles struggles with expectations but ultimately embraces courage, heart, and the belief that anyone can wear the mask.
Peter B. Parker

“You won’t know it’s the right moment. That’s why it’s a leap of faith.”

An older, worn-out Spider-Man from another dimension who’s lost his confidence — and his way. Despite his flaws, he becomes an unexpected mentor to Miles and rediscovers what it means to be a hero.
Gwen Stacy

“Don’t do it like me. Do it like you.”

A skilled and confident Spider-Woman who keeps people at a distance after losing someone close. Gwen hides her pain behind strength but learns to trust again through her bond with Miles and the team.
Spider-Man Noir

“We don’t choose the ballroom. We just dance.”

A black-and-white detective-style Spider-Man from a 1930s universe. Stoic, brooding, and dramatic, he brings a gritty perspective — yet shows that even the toughest heroes can care deeply.
Peni Parker

“You’re the best of all of us, Miles. You’re on your way.”

A tech-savvy girl from the future who pilots a robotic suit linked to a radioactive spider. Cheerful and intelligent, Peni blends heart with high-tech heroism and offers Miles kindness and encouragement.
Spider-Ham

“That's all, folks."

A cartoon pig with Spider-Man abilities, mixing humor with unexpected bravery. Though silly on the surface, he proves that heroism comes in all forms — even animated ones.
